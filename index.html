01

What is
this challenge?

You'll face 12 real leadership situations drawn directly from conversations with leaders at HLE/Alaya — the exact problems they described facing day-to-day. You choose how many to play when you start.

For each situation, you'll pick how you'd respond. After everyone picks, you'll see what actually happens as a result — and why.

At the end, you'll get a Leadership Profile showing your strengths and where to grow next.

💡 In multiplayer mode: pass the device around so each person picks privately. After all players choose, you'll reveal your answers at the same time — which is where the real discussion starts.

03

The Framework
Behind the Scores

🛡️
Trust — max 21 pts
Based on Patrick Lencioni's Five Dysfunctions of a Team and Brené Brown's vulnerability-based trust research. The question: does your team feel safe enough to be honest with you?
👁️
Proactivity — max 23 pts
Covey's "Be Proactive" habit combined with Goleman's Emotional Intelligence research — specifically social awareness: noticing what's happening in your team before anyone says it out loud.
🎯
Empowerment — max 19 pts
Hersey & Blanchard's Situational Leadership model. Real delegation means transferring the why, not just the what — and trusting people to make it better than you would have.
🔄
Adaptability — max 21 pts
John Kotter's change leadership model and McKinsey's adaptive leadership research. The best leaders don't just survive change — they help their team move through it with momentum.

⚠️ No "perfect" score exists. Every scenario rewards different dimensions. Real leadership always involves tradeoffs — that's the point.

04

How to play
together

Once you add players, here's what happens for each round:

👤
Step 1 — Each player picks privately
The device is passed around. Each person reads the situation and picks their response without seeing what others chose.
🎭
Step 2 — Reveal all at once
After everyone picks, you hit Reveal together. Seeing where your instincts differ — or align — is the most interesting part.
📖
Step 3 — Read the outcome
Each choice has a real consequence explained in full. Don't skip this — it's where the learning happens.
📊
Step 4 — Check the scoreboard
After each round, see how everyone's dimensions are tracking. Then move to the next scenario.
